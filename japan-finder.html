Japan Rail Pass Tips
Do NOT take Nozomi or Mizuho ShinkansenThese are the fastest bullet trains but your JR Pass is NOT valid on them. Always take Hikari or Sakura instead — only slightly slower.
✅
What is covered
All JR bullet trains except Nozomi/Mizuho, local JR trains, JR buses, and the Narita Express (N'EX) from Narita airport to Tokyo.
🎫
Book reserved seats early
Reserved seats are free with your pass. Book at any JR ticket office as soon as you arrive. Tokyo to Osaka fills up fast.
🚉
Your key routes
Tokyo to Osaka: Hikari Shinkansen (~3h). Osaka to Kyoto: JR Kyoto Line (~15 min). Kyoto to Tokyo: Hikari Shinkansen (~2h15). All covered.
✈️
Airport trains
Narita to Tokyo: N'EX covered by pass. Haneda to Tokyo: NOT covered — take Tokyo Monorail (~500 yen) or Keikyu Line.
📅
Activate wisely
Exchange your voucher at Haneda or Narita on arrival. Choose your activation date carefully — the pass runs for consecutive days so activate it when you plan to start travelling between cities.
💰
Is it worth it?
Tokyo to Osaka alone costs ~13,870 yen one way. A 14-day Ordinary pass is 50,000 yen — your itinerary includes Tokyo, Osaka and Kyoto round trips so it pays for itself easily.
